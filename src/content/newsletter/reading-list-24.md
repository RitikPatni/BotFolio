Hello Everyone,

Thanks to all the new folks who have subscribed and the already existing ones to have stayed this far and kept me going. And with all that, let's start with this week's interesting stuff.

## 📚 To Read

[The Road to Burnout is Paved With Context Switching](http://sophieshepherd.com/2017/06/14/learning-to-be-chill.html?utm_source=ritik&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=reading-list-24)

I came across this post from 5 years ago and loved going through it. I'll recommend reading this if you're also in a burnout stage in your career.

[I only care about the helpful notifications, not the promotional ones](https://alexanderell.is/posts/sneaking-notifications/?utm_source=ritik&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=reading-list-24)

This constantly happens and yet, there is no way to stop this. I hope developers and marketing managers take note of these things and improve user's experience, but until that happens, you can definitely read this post and relate this with your issues.

[After Being Banned from More than Ten Social Media Sites, I Decided to Create My Own](https://cheapskatesguide.org/articles/my-social-media-site.html?utm_source=ritik&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=reading-list-24)

The title sums up pretty much all I could've added. A pretty nice read and a really cool story that will keep you hooked.

[I've locked myself out of my digital life](https://shkspr.mobi/blog/2022/06/ive-locked-myself-out-of-my-digital-life/?utm_source=ritik&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=reading-list-24)

This one made me think how much of “Code is Law” philosophy can be truly implemented. Agreed, this is an imaginary scenario only, but all of this can actually happen in really life as well, and it made me think about my choices about password management and all other digital things.

[LGBTQ+ Flags Coded in CSS](https://alvaromontoro.com/blog/68009/lgbtq-flags-coded-in-css?utm_source=ritik&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=reading-list-24)

June is Pride Month and here's a bit of code to raise awareness about pride flags and also share some impressive little single element pure CSS snippets.

[8 Shocking HTML tips you probably don't know about](https://dev.to/babib/7-shocking-html-tips-you-probably-dont-know-about-ggd?utm_source=ritik&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=reading-list-24)

Many TIL moments in this one. Will recommend reading it if you deal with HTML very frequently.

[CSS DAY — day 1](https://grrr.tech/posts/2022/css-day-day-1/?utm_source=ritik&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=reading-list-24)

CSS Day is over now, but there were so many wonderful things that happened there. It's sad that there was no way we could've joined it without being physically present there, but these notes are a nice summary of it and contains so many remarkable things inside it. [CSS DAY — day 2](https://grrr.tech/posts/2022/css-day-day-2/?utm_source=ritik&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=reading-list-24)
