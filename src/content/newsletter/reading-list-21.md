## 📚 To Read

[Not so short note on aria-label usage – Big Table Edition](https://html5accessibility.com/stuff/2020/11/07/not-so-short-note-on-aria-label-usage-big-table-edition/?utm_source=ritik&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=reading-list-21)

This was a very concise and informative read on aria-label. I got to learn a lot from this and if you're working with web accessibility in any way or form I'll recommend reading this.

[Leaky Forms: A Study of Email and Password Exfiltration Before Form Submission](https://homes.esat.kuleuven.be/~asenol/leaky-forms/?utm_source=ritik&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=reading-list-21)

This scares me a lot. I wish more companies cared about the data we put in their website, but to see so many prominent websites just leaking your personal email and password even before you hit that submit button is scary.

[[In Search of Organic Software](https://pketh.org/organic-software.html)](https://pketh.org/organic-software.html?utm_source=ritik&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=reading-list-21)

Loved reading this, and I believe if you plan to start a SaaS business then you should be clear whether you will be open to VCs or have a clear business model(something I really like about [Buttondown](https://buttondown.email/?utm_source=ritik&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=reading-list-21)). Not having either and relying on people's good will is I believe a definitive way to ensure you won't be serving your users the experience they want for a long enough period.

[The Surprising Truth About Pixels and Accessibility](https://www.joshwcomeau.com/css/surprising-truth-about-pixels-and-accessibility/?utm_source=ritik&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=reading-list-21)

So many good points and considerations shared by Josh in this one. Can't recommend this enough if you work with CSS or websites.

[I spent 2 years launching tiny projects](https://tinyprojects.dev/posts/i_spent_two_years_launching_tiny_projects?utm_source=ritik&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=reading-list-21)

This one is interesting and loved reading about the journey. I've thinking along these lines, but never tried it yet. I'll probably try this soon and would love to experience how it turns out to be.

[Unblocking Your Writing Blocks, Part 3: I Can’t Write](https://matthiasott.com/notes/unblocking-your-writing-blocks-part-3-i-cant-write?utm_source=ritik&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=reading-list-21)

Like the last 2 posts in the series, this one is again a good one. I'll recommend reading all 3 if you haven't already managed to do that.([Part 1](https://matthiasott.com/notes/unblocking-your-writing-blocks-01?utm_source=ritik&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=reading-list-21) [Part2](https://matthiasott.com/notes/unblocking-your-writing-blocks-part-2-im-not-an-expert?utm_source=ritik&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=reading-list-21))

[Daydreaming is better than doomscrolling](https://gilest.org/daydreaming-doomscrolling.html?utm_source=ritik&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=reading-list-21)

This is another good suggestion about reading habits and definitely going to try this as I've been a procrastinator on this for the past few months and I want to definitely fix that.

[You Should Flip a Coin](https://kevq.uk/you-should-flip-a-coin/?utm_source=ritik&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=reading-list-21)

Good advice this is a good idea to try out if you can't come up to a decision rationally on something but have to opt for one option.
