Hello everyone,Finally, back to writing this one. Last week was wonderful and a pretty interesting one. I've been travelling the whole last week through the mountains, and hence this issue got delayed. So without wasting much time, let's get started with last week's exciting stuff.

## 📚 To Read

[IBM's asshole test](https://johnpublic.mataroa.blog/blog/the-asshole-test/?utm_source=ritik&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=reading-list-22)This was an interesting read about entry processes inside IBM's grad scheme

[Blogging Streaks & Freaks](https://tomcritchlow.com/2022/05/20/streaks/?utm_source=ritik&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=reading-list-22)A lot of wise advice about blogging and how to do more of it.

[The balance has shifted away from SPAs](https://nolanlawson.com/2022/05/21/the-balance-has-shifted-away-from-spas/?utm_source=ritik&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=reading-list-22)So many new things for MPAs which means less dependency overall on building SPAs and more people can opt into this without having to trade off features from either type of web applications.

[Is DuckDuckGo, DuckDuckDone?](https://kevq.uk/is-duckduckgo-duckduckdone/?utm_source=ritik&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=reading-list-22)DuckDuckGo have recently been found allowing Microsoft trackers through their browsers, and this feels disappointing considering the stance DuckDuckGo have always held on to.

[Keep the web free, say no to Web3](https://yesterweb.org/no-to-web3/?utm_source=ritik&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=reading-list-22)

Web3 has been called many things: an idea, a movement, a new iteration of the internet.

It’s the ‘future’ of the internet, proposed by [cryptocurrency enthusiasts](https://www.sfchronicle.com/tech/article/Crypto-has-a-bro-problem-Women-in-the-field-say-16336453.php?utm_source=ritik&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=reading-list-22), based on public blockchain.

This is a no-hype, down-to-earth breakdown of everything we have learned about Web3.

[Just How Long Should Alt Text Be?](https://css-tricks.com/just-how-long-should-alt-text-be/?utm_source=ritik&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=reading-list-22)I always keep getting confused with this thing and this was a lot helpful to clear up my mind.
