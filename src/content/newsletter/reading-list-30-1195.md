Hello Everyone,

Hope everyone is doing great and had a wonderful week. Mine was a quiet one, as I'm still waiting on my hardware to arrive so that I can get started. With that said, let's get started with this week's interesting stuff…

## 📚 To Read

[Finding Stuff to Write About](https://blog.jim-nielsen.com/2022/finding-stuff-to-write-about/?utm_source=ritik&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=reading-list-30-1195)

Some useful advice if you struggle with finding stuff to write about.

[Technical Writing for Developers](https://css-tricks.com/technical-writing-for-developers/?utm_source=ritik&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=reading-list-30-1195)

Pretty useful guidelines for technical writing, which you are eventually going to need if you're writing code.

[Planting trees isn’t enough. Here’s why we need tiny man-made forests](https://www.inverse.com/science/mini-forests?utm_source=ritik&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=reading-list-30-1195)

Fascinating read on how to develop sustainable ecosystems compared to planting trees in bulk of same species

[Saying thank you](https://ericwbailey.design/writing/saying-thank-you/?utm_source=ritik&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=reading-list-30-1195)

A little gratitude or a small thank-you email is more than enough to make someone's day.

[learning to tolerate physiological discomfort](https://winnielim.org/journal/learning-to-tolerate-physiological-discomfort/?utm_source=ritik&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=reading-list-30-1195)

Interesting read on physiological discomfort and it's benefits

[Inspiration is perishable; Act on it immediately.](https://www.sarasoueidan.com/desk/inspiration-is-perishable/?utm_source=ritik&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=reading-list-30-1195)

Excellent advice from Sara Soueidan on why not to wait on something if you have a good inspiration

[Abbreviations can be problematic](https://www.tempertemper.net/blog/abbreviations-can-be-problematic?utm_source=ritik&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=reading-list-30-1195)

Abbreviations can be problematic and will always be if your audience don't have the same background as you, which is highly likely and is the main reason why should try not to use them or explain them first if you are still going to end up using them
